Improving self-service for travel certificates

Responsive web + mobile app

Role

Lead Product Designer

Client

United Airlines

Timeline

4 weeks

Deliverables

Flow mapping, prototypes, concept validation testing, visual design mockups, accessibility documentation

Background

United issues electronic travel certificates, that can be applied towards future travel with the airline, as a form of compensation for a disservice event or as a goodwill gesture. With the COVID-19 pandemic contributing to a sharp increase in certificates being issued, integrating digital self-service for electronic travel certificates became a top priority.

Reference: United travel credits

Challenge

As the main compensation method for customers, travel certificates are deeply integrated into the airline’s frontline operations and enterprise technology tools. With the unprecedented volume of travel certificates in rotation, this was an urgent enterprise-wide request to address a situation with high public visibility. As the Lead Product Designer, I rapidly designed, tested, and iterated how to integrate digital travel certificates into United’s customer-facing website and mobile app.

Existing state

As certificates are issued directly to travelers via print or email, and not integrated into United’s digital channels, a top driver of customer calls were "where to find" and "how to use certificates.”
