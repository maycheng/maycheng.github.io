Digitizing self-service for travel certificates

Responsive web + mobile app

Role

Lead Product Designer

Client

United Airlines

Timeline

4 weeks

Deliverables

Flow mapping, information architecture, wireframes, prototypes, concept validation testing, visual design mockups, accessibility documentation

Background

United issues Electronic travel certificates (ETCs) to customers as a form of compensation that is redeemable for future travel with the airline. As certificates are only issued via print and email with limited and difficult to find information on how to use them, customers are often driven to call the contact center for help.

Reference: United travel credits

Challenge

With COVID-19’s impact to travel changes, 2019 was shaping up to be a record year for ETC issuance. There was an enterprise-wide focus on integrating travel certificates into the airline’s digital channels to help reduce contact center requests during a time of unprecedented call volumes. Given that half of certificates issued are associated to a MileagePlus account, the initial area of focus was for United members. As the Lead Product Designer, I quickly gathered research, tested concepts and delivered designs to integrate Electronic travel certificates into member accounts on the United website and mobile app.

Existing state

As certificates are issued directly to travelers via print or email, and not integrated into United’s digital channels, a top driver of customer calls were "where to find" and "how to use certificates.”
